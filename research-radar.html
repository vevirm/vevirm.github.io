Research Radar

Recent work worth noticing — and the background readings already around my work.

This page collects high-quality work in fields close to my research: futures studies, foresight, Delphi, narrative foresight, futures of work, university futures, philosophy of science, historiography, counterfactuals, and related areas.

It is selective rather than exhaustive. The aim is not to follow everything new, but to notice work that gives useful concepts, methods, arguments, or evidence.

Songs, films, sayings

Small pieces of wisdom for large claims about futures

A place for short lines from songs, films, and public culture that can irritate, sharpen, or humble foresight talk. The point is not quotation collecting; the point is commentary.

“You don’t need a weatherman...”

Bob Dylan, “Subterranean Homesick Blues” (1965)

If foresight people start behaving as weather-men, oh my gosh. The useful question is what foresight adds when the wind is already felt: better framing, better consequences, better contestation, and better attention to who pretends not to notice.

What belongs here?

Peer-reviewed articles, serious academic books, book chapters, and major research reports. The work should help thinking, method development, conceptual clarification, or empirical understanding.

What does not belong here?

Generic trend lists, normal news pieces, thin horizon reports, and weak “future of X” speculation. Newness alone is not enough.
