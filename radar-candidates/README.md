# Research Radar candidates

This folder is generated by `.github/workflows/research-radar.yml`.

`latest.md` is the file to review after each weekly scan. The scanner is conservative about publication quality but flexible about intellectual neighbourhood: candidates must first pass a hard publication-type gate and a substance gate, then show a real connection to the site's futures/philosophy/methods/system-level themes before scoring. Self-authored works are excluded from discovery results.

The gate admits only:

- DOI-bearing scholarly journal articles;
- scholarly books and edited-volume chapters from whitelisted academic publishers;
- institutional, policy, and sector reports from recognised organisations;
- borderline preprints or working papers only when they come from a recognised research group or repository and have enough methodological/substantive signals.

It excludes news, blog posts, vendor/consultancy marketing, press releases, listicles, shallow trend roundups, encyclopedic pages, course materials, weak/thin outputs, and high-quality-but-off-topic empirical papers that do not connect to the site's futures/philosophy/methodological/system-level focus.

Tune the gate in `assets/research-radar-config.json`, especially `minimum_score`, `days_back`, `fit_gate`, `site_focus`, `self_exclusion`, `quality_gate.min_substance_signals`, the domain whitelists, and the exclusion lists.


## Focus gate

The Radar is intentionally centred on Veli Virmajoki's research neighbourhood rather than on every scholarly paper that uses words such as "future", "narrative", "work", or "scenario". A good candidate should normally do at least one of these:

- develop or evaluate a futures/foresight method;
- clarify concepts such as possibility, plausibility, anticipation, weak signals, narratives, scenarios, Delphi, or Causal Layered Analysis;
- contribute to philosophy of futures studies, philosophy of science, historiography, counterfactual reasoning, conceivability, or futures of science;
- analyse futures of work, universities, or higher education at the level of systems, institutions, arrangements, policy, knowledge, or governance.

A paper can be methodologically serious and still be rejected if it is mainly a micro-experience study in an unrelated domain. But the gate should not require exact copies of the site's own titles: adjacent work on methods, concepts, governance, science systems, work arrangements, university futures, counterfactual reasoning, and anticipatory governance is allowed. Tune this in `site_focus`, `fit_gate`, and `topics` inside `assets/research-radar-config.json`.
