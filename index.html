Academic profile

What I do

I work at the intersection of futures studies, philosophy of science, and philosophy of historiography. My research examines how we reason about possibility, time, explanation, contingency, and alternative futures.

I have published peer-reviewed work in futures studies and philosophy journals, authored a monograph on causal explanation in historiography, and contributed to research on futures of work, universities, science, and research careers.

What I work toward

Scientifically serious futures research

My aim is to strengthen futures studies as a research field that is conceptually clear, methodologically careful, and useful for organizations and society. I am interested in foresight that does not merely produce attractive scenarios, but improves how people understand real possibilities, choices, and consequences.
